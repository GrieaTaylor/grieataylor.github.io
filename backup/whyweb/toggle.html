Supply Chain
The life of your cotton t-shirt
FARMER



• Farmer Plants cotton seeds



• Cotton is cleaned, ginned and checked for quality



• Cotton is sold and transported to a mill



FACTORY



• Cotton is spun in yarn, then woven into fabric



• Fabric is sent to a finishing plant



• It is bleached, pre-shrunk, dyed, printed and treated



• Fabric is assessed for quality then sent to garment factory, if faulty or wrong shade it is disposed of



• Garment is cut by hands and tools, 60 billion meters of fabric are wasted on the cutting room floor each year



• Garment is sewn by workers and machine



• Trimmings and details are added



• Garment is washed and checked for quality control



• Garment is cleaned then pressed



TRANSPORT



• Garment is packaged and stored for shipment



• Garment is sent to a distribution warehouse



• Garment is sent to store by truck



SALE



• Garment is unpacked and placed on the shop floor



• You buy the garment



• Garment is out of stock. Brand requests for more of the stock but this time cheaper



YOU



• You wear it and wash it



• You get a stain on the tshirt and apply strong chemicals to it



• It's winter, you dry it in a heat dryer



• It gets a hole in it



• You donate it



• The second hand shop deems it unsellable



• It goes to landfill



Microfibers from fabrics wind up in the ocean and threaten aquatic life
